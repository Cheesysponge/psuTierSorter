Tier meaning

Quick guidance on what each tier is for.

A-, A, A+
  • High quality units
  • Typically 5+ year warranty
  • Strong third‑party testing / platforms
B-, B, B+
  • Good for most systems
  • Usually 3+ year warranty
  • Solid value picks
C-, C, C+
  • Tighter budgets / older builds
  • Check exact model & wattage
  • Avoid for high‑end GPUs if possible
D-, D, D+
  • Very tight budget
  • Often not recommended
  • Use only when you fully understand tradeoffs

FAQ

Most of the links are Amazon Affiliates from SPLs tierlist since their group did the majority of the work gathering the data. I recommend using the links here instead of using PCPP to support us.
The finder scrapes hundreds of powersupplies and compares them to thousands on SPLs PSU tierlist. This is a difficult process and many powersupplies have different variants with the exact same name so I recommend taking these results with a grain of salt.
The tier ratings are from SPLs PSU tierlist more info about what these rankings mean are found over there.
I have been given explicit permission from SPL himself, with him saying "If you can find a way to aggregate the data without requiring edit access, sure."
