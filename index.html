Tier Meaning

A reference for what each tier is for

A-,A,A+

High Quality Units.

High End

  • Minimum 5 year warranty
  • Undergone high quality testing
  • Ex: 3090, 4070ti, 5080, 7900xt, etc

B-,B,B+

Decent quality PSU

Most Systems

  • Minimum 3 year warranty(not if -)
  • Decent third party reviews
  • Ex: 4060 ti-5070, 9060xt-9070xt, etc

C-,C,C+

For people on a tight budget/older PC

Budget/Older

  • Minimum 3 year warranty(not if -)
  • Minimum quality worth using for most people
  • Ex: 2070-4060, rx 5700xt, 6650xt etc

D-,D,D+

Very Very Tight Budget

iGPU builds only

  • Minimum 3 year warranty(not if -)
  • Generally Unrecommended
  • Shouldn't be used with a dedicated GPU

Can I trust the recommendations?

The finder scrapes hundreds of powersupplies and compares them to thousands on SPLs PSU tierlist. This is a difficult process and many powersupplies have different variants with the exact same name so I recommend taking these results with a grain of salt.

Where did you get the tier ratings from and are you allowed to use them?

The tier ratings are from SPLs PSU tierlist more info about what these rankings mean are found over there.
I have been explicit given permission from SPL himself, with him saying "If you can find a way to aggregate the data without requiring edit access, sure."
